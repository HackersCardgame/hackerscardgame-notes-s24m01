The amygdala also corpus amygdaloideum; Latin,
from Greek duvy6aAn, is one of two almond-
shaped groups of nuclei located deep and medially
within the temporal lobes of the brain in complex
vertebrates, including humans. Shown in research
to perform a primary role in the processing of
memory, decision-making, and emotional
reactions, the amygdalae are considered part of
the limbic system.[3]

Use Cases

Development / processing of anxiety, fear and
anger, "fight or flight" - understanding or
manipulating reaction, "rage"
