In celebration of the 250th anniversary of the birth of G.W.F. Hegel, Slavoj Zizek gives us a reading of the philosophical giant that
changes our way of thinking about our new posthuman era. No ordinary study of Hegel, Hegel in a Wired Brain investigates what he
might have had to say about the idea of the ‘wired brain’ — what happens when a direct link between our mental processes and a
digital machine emerges. Zizek explores the phenomenon of a wired brain effect, and what might happen when we can share our
thoughts directly with others. He hones in on the key question of how it shapes our experience and status as ‘free' individuals and
‘asks what it means to be human when a machine can read our minds.

With characteristic verve and enjoyment of the unexpected, ZiZek connects Hegel to the world we live in now, shows why he is much
more fun than anyone gives him credit for, and why the 21st century might just be Hegelian.
